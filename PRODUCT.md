# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Baloo is for **everyday, health-curious shoppers** — ordinary people standing in a supermarket (or browsing its site) who want to understand what's actually in a product, without being scored, ranked, or scared. They are not clinicians or nutrition experts; they want plain language and a trustworthy read, quickly. Their context is a real buying decision or a moment of curiosity about something already in the cupboard. A secondary, emergent audience are **curators** — the same shoppers who start building and sharing lists — but when their needs conflict, the everyday reader wins: clarity and calm before power-user features.

## Product Purpose

Baloo turns a supermarket product into a calm, plain-language breakdown of every ingredient — **what each thing is, and why it's in this specific product** — and then lets people save those products into shareable lists. A person pastes a product link (or searches products Baloo already knows), reads the per-ingredient explanation and a neutral nutrition view, and saves it to a list they can publish and share. Underneath, it is becoming a community food-discovery platform: lists are the primary object, people follow curators, and Baloo's explanation sits under every item. Success is a **habit and a community** — people return to build and share lists, rather than scanning one product and leaving.

## Brand Personality

Calm, knowledgeable, trustworthy — a well-read friend who happens to understand food labels, not a clinic and not a scanner. The voice educates before it persuades, is never alarmist, and chooses context over judgment. Warmth and credibility carry the brand; the interface should feel unhurried and considered, the kind of place you trust because it never tries to scare or sell you.

## Anti-references

Baloo must not feel like a **clinical or medical app** — sterile, chart-heavy, warning-laden, the mood of a health record rather than a friend. And it must never become **alarmist "toxic ingredient" fear content** — scare tactics, "avoid these 5 poisons," fear used as engagement. Both betray the calm, educational promise.

## Design Principles

**Explain, never judge.** Baloo has no score, no rating, no 0–100, no traffic lights, ever — it explains what's in food and lets the reader decide. This is the product's soul and the single rule every screen protects.

**Colour is meaning, not decoration.** Green and amber appear only as the small Natural/Processed ingredient tags; nowhere else carries loud colour. Restraint is what makes the one meaningful signal legible.

**One analysis, reused everywhere.** A product is a reusable object and its ingredient breakdown is generated once and attached wherever the product appears. The explanation is the value — never regenerated per view, never faked.

**Community brings opinions; Baloo brings facts.** The AI is something you *ask*, on a product or a list — it never lectures, never authors the lists, and stays visibly distinct from human voices.

**Lists are the primary object.** Discovery happens through what people curate and trust, not through isolated one-off scans; the design should make building and sharing feel effortless and the read-under-every-item ever-present.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: 4.5:1 body-text contrast, full keyboard access with visible focus, and honoured reduced-motion (already the default in `globals.css`). The Natural/Processed tags always carry a text label, so meaning never depends on colour alone — important given the green/amber pair. Assume a broad range of everyday devices and screen sizes; responsive behaviour is structural, and nothing critical should require hover or precise pointing.
